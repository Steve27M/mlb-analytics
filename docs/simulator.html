How to read it: we replay the rest of the season 10,000 times, letting every remaining game land by its probability — like rolling weighted dice for every game, thousands of seasons in a row. A team's playoff odds are simply: in how many of those seasons did it get in? It's a season-long projection, not a pick for any single game. Remaining games use each team's current form, so the odds lean on where teams stand today; a hot or cold streak will move them.
Why the projections huddle together
Projected win totals cluster toward the middle not because teams are equal, but because luck averages out across 10,000 replays while a single real season keeps its luck. The range shown under each projection is the honest part: about 8 seasons in 10 land inside it.
Does it actually work?
Rerunning a finished season this way put 10 of the 12 real playoff teams in our top 12 — good, and the two misses are the point: some of every season is genuinely unpredictable. See how the model was validated.
