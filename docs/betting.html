Is the edge real?

The game model is benchmarked against a home-field baseline and a Pythagorean baseline — it beats both on Brier — not against closing lines. Beating the closing line is the actual bar for a profitable model, and we don't claim it.

Calibration (for pricing)

When the model says 55%, does it happen 55% of the time? For pricing, calibration matters more than accuracy.

Predictions sit in a tight 0.39–0.669 band (a thin edge = few confident calls), but within it, stated probability tracks the observed rate — points hug the dashed diagonal. 5 equal-count buckets, n per point.
Variance & sizing reality
In plain terms: when we say 60%, it happens about 60% of the time, and our probabilities are slightly sharper than always-leaning-home (Brier 0.247 vs 0.249). Thin — but thin edges are the only kind that exist in this market, and they only pay at volume and with discipline (Funder & Ozer, 2019: small effects compound).
Odds, translated: a 65% win probability is −186 — risk $186 to win $100. That is the price the model implies; a posted line bakes in the book's margin on top.
